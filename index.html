Quantum Mechanics Quantum Mechanics I & II

A comprehensive introduction to non-relativistic quantum mechanics — from the postulates of quantum theory through perturbation methods, angular momentum, and scattering theory.

Topics covered:

  • Wave mechanics, Schrödinger equation, and probability interpretation
  • Operator methods, Dirac notation, and Hilbert spaces
  • Harmonic oscillator, hydrogen atom, angular momentum
  • Time-independent and time-dependent perturbation theory
  • Variational method and WKB approximation
  • Identical particles and spin statistics

Condensed Matter Introduction to Condensed Matter Physics

From crystal structure and lattice vibrations to band theory, magnetism, and superconductivity — a self-contained introduction to the physics of solids.

  • Crystal structure, reciprocal lattice, Brillouin zones
  • Phonons and lattice dynamics
  • Free electron model and band theory
  • Semiconductors, metals, and insulators
  • Magnetism: dia-, para-, ferro-, antiferromagnetism
  • Superconductivity: BCS theory and phenomenology
Lecture Notes (PDF) Problem Sets
Statistical Mechanics Statistical Mechanics & Thermodynamics

Foundations of equilibrium and non-equilibrium statistical mechanics, connecting microscopic laws to macroscopic thermodynamics.

  • Microcanonical, canonical, and grand canonical ensembles
  • Partition functions and free energies
  • Quantum statistics: Fermi–Dirac and Bose–Einstein distributions
  • Phase transitions and critical phenomena
  • Introduction to non-equilibrium statistical mechanics

Mathematical Methods Mathematical Methods for Physicists

Essential mathematical tools for theoretical physics — from complex analysis and special functions to group theory and differential equations.

  • Linear algebra and vector spaces
  • Complex analysis and contour integration
  • Differential equations and Green's functions
  • Special functions (Legendre, Bessel, Hermite, etc.)
  • Introduction to group theory for physicists
